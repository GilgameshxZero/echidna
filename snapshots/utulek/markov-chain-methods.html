Markov chain methods

May 31, 2023

The general methods of solving first-time-to-state or absorption-likelihood-from-state are easy to remember.

That is, to solve for the expected time until first entering a state sss, we compute variables xix_ixi​ signifiying the expected time until entering state sss, when starting from state iii. Then, necessarily, this reduces to a linear algebra problem with nnn as the total number of states.

Similarily, to compute the expected time until absorption at some component or node, we may compute variables xix_ixi​ for the same metric, when starting at state iii, and solve a linear system.

Question: Consider not just the mean, but the distribution of time until absorption or first time to state, starting from some state sss. What is this distribution?

Unbounded Markov walk/Gambler’s ruin

In the classical Gambler’s ruin question, we ask:

Currently we are at state 111. At state 000 is an absorption node we call loss. With probability ppp we transition from some state sss to s+1s+1s+1, and with probability 1−p1-p1−p we transition to s−1s-1s−1. What is the likelihood of absorption from every state?

The crucial observation in this problem is that absorption from state 222 requires us to travel to 111 first, then be absorbed from 111. That is, if the likelihood of absorption from 111 is x1x_1x1​, then x2=x12x_2=x_1^2x2​=x12​. But we also have

x1=1−p+px2  ⟹  px12−x1+1−p=0x_1=1-p+px_2\implies px_1^2-x_1+1-p=0 x1​=1−p+px2​⟹px12​−x1​+1−p=0

which is a solvable quadratic with solutions

x1=1±1−4p+4p2/(2p)=1,(1−p)/p.x_1=1\pm\sqrt{1-4p+4p^2}/(2p)=1,(1-p)/p. x1​=1±1−4p+4p2​/(2p)=1,(1−p)/p.

So we know that this analysis is only valid if p≤0.5p\leq 0.5p≤0.5. Luckily, oftentimes we are able to switch the directions of ppp and 1−p1-p1−p to establish this, and on the border we have p=0.5p=0.5p=0.5 and xi=1x_i=1xi​=1 for all iii.

This is a fine problem, and may be modeled by the naive Markov chain approach as well, but with some difficulty in dealing with infinite series. We now extend this problem to have two absorbing states at 000 and n+mn+mn+m. The starting state is now nnn, with the same probability ppp of moving right.

I bring up now a brief argument why absorption must eventually occur when there are two boundaries. Consider that each step is a Bernoulli RV of ±1\pm1±1, and thus their sum is binomial, with variance tending to ∞\infty∞ as the number of steps also tends to ∞\infty∞. Thus, the mass of the binomial RV that is between 000 and n+mn+mn+m must vanish, and hence absorption must occur with likelihood 111 as steps tends to ∞\infty∞.

As before, we may compute the likelihood of loss x1=(1−p)/px_1=(1-p)/px1​=(1−p)/p. Notably, should p<0.5p<0.5p<0.5, we may instead define loss at n+mn+mn+m and thus compute the likelihood of loss at xn+m−1x_{n+m-1}xn+m−1​. We then consider the likelihood of left-absorption lnl_nln​ from starting state nnn (the argument must be reversed for p<0.5p<0.5p<0.5):

ln=xn−(1−ln)xn+m,l_n=x_n-(1-l_n)x_{n+m}, ln​=xn​−(1−ln​)xn+m​,

that is, to be left-absorbed is to lose (xnx_nxn​) without being right-absorbed first. The likelihood of being right-absorbed first is (1−ln)(1-l_n)(1−ln​) (the likelihood of not being left-absorbed first, since absorption must happen) times xn+mx_{n+m}xn+m​ (losing after becoming right-absorbed).

And thus we may solve for lnl_nln​, remembering that xi=x1ix_i=x_1^ixi​=x1i​.

There is little more to be said about this problem.

MCMC

Markov Chain Monte Carlo is frankly a trivial method to estimate the stationary distribution of a Markov chain. Basically, we run simulations, and stop at some point, and let the estimator of the stationary distribution be the probability derived from these multiple simulations.

It must be asked: what properties does the MCMC estimator have? Is it unbiased or consistent? Consistency is intuitively obvious, while unbiased-ness should only arise when the starting state distribution is unbiased. But otherwise, it seems like a fairly safe procedure.

It remains to be determined how many steps the simulation should be run.
